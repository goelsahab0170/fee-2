The Pulitzer Prize - Literary Award

The Pulitzer Prize is an annual award that recognizes outstanding work in journalism, literature, and music.

The prize was established in 1917 by provisions in the will of Joseph Pulitzer, a Hungarian-born newspaper publisher who made his fortune in the United States.

The Pulitzer Prize is considered one of the most prestigious awards in American literature, and winners are chosen by a board of judges from Columbia University.
